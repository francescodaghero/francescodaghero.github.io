I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at University of Southern Denmark, where I am part of the DECO research group. I received both my M.Sc. degree in Computer Engineering (October 25, 2019) and my Ph.D. (May 21, 2024) from Politecnico di Torino. Following the completion of my PhD, I continued at Politecnico di Torino as a postdoctoral researcher from 2024 to the end of 2025 before joining SDU.

Over the years, I have also been a visiting researcher at the STMicroelectronics headquarters in Prague through my involvement in a Marie Curie European research project.

My research focuses on Machine Learning and Deep Learning, with particular emphasis on energy- and memory-efficient inference. My expertise spans the full deployment stack, from high-level algorithmic optimization to compiler-level techniques for efficient AI systems. More recently, my work has expanded toward efficient deployment and applications of Small Language Models at the edge.

To date, I have co-authored more than 15 scientific publications, including six journal articles and two book chapters. I have also contributed to five European-funded research projects and one Danish-funded project.

I am always open to new collaborations or to discuss about thesis projects, feel free to contact me via email for a chat.
